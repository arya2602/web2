ABOUT CEC

Competitive Exam Cell (CEC) is a student driven initiative at JSPM’s Rajarshi Shahu College of Engineering, Tathawade, Pune. To create awareness regarding Competitive Exams and to create a Competitive Exam friendly environment is the prime objective of Competitive Exam Cell.

Competitive Exam Cell provides a platform to the students where they can take ownership and get acquainted with the leadership skills. CEC is open for anyone preparing for any Competitive Exam regardless of the Technical and Non-Technical background of the Exam.

ABOUT RSCOE

Rajarshi Shahu College of Engineering is one among the hallmark institutes of pune, established in 2001 with the aim of providing quality technical education and excellence in the ever expanding horizon of technical revolution of 21st century.

The institute is becoming an icon in the field of engineering education in the state of Maharashtra. It is nurtured and managed by Jayawant Shikshan Prasarak Manadal Trust. The Institute is approved by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), New Delhi and Govt. of Maharashtra affiliated to Savitribai Phule Pune University.

OUR EVENTS

Our Grand Event !!

“COMPITITIVE EXAM CONCLAVE”

Competitive Exam Cell Completed it's one year So, Competitive Exam Conclave was organized on 28th and 29th of April 2023 At JSPM's Rajarshi Shahu College of Engineering.

Competitive Exam Conclave has been inaugurated by our esteemed Chief Guest, Dr. Ravi Joshi , Director JSPM Group. His presence has added immense value to the event and we are grateful for his guidance.

During the inauguration, Dr. Ravi Joshi unveiled the magazine "Shakti" and the code of conduct "Nitishastra" of the Competitive Exam Cell. Magazine consist of report of all activities, visits, sources developed , achievements in the whole year and future planned activities. On the other hand, "Nitishastra" is the code of conduct of CEC. It is the guidelines and a set of ethical standards that the members of team must adhere to while working in team.

Aspirants always have number of doubt related to exam ,self which where covered in Competitive Exam Round Table. To get the guidance on discussed problems during round table we arranged expert talk 'Competitive Exam : KSA' by Dr. Anurag Singh. There he explained how syllabus of any competitive exam is linked with aspirants. Also he stated that wherever you wish or aims to work, you must visit that place.

On day 2 of the competitive exam conclave, different activities were conducted with the purpose of bringing students together from various backgrounds to engage in discussions focused on competitive exam preparation and to develop OLQ's.

Starting with 1st Activity PPDT (picture perception & discussion test) which is an important element of SSB interview process was conducted. Here different perspectives of a similar picture were highlighted. Also, one of the students Shubham Mahajan who recently appeared for SSB interview process guided on same to all participants.

Followed by this 2nd activity SRT (Situation Reaction Test) which is also an important element of SSB interview process was conducted. Here the ability of an individual to react to a particular situation was highlighted.

After that 3rd activity Debate was held in continuation where different topics were covered and both sides of topic were understood. This debate was judged by a panel of 2 judges. At the end judges given feedbacks and shared their experience.

We had Dr. P.P Vitkar as Chief guest who is having experience of 60 years in field of education, for valedictory ceremony of Competitive Exam Conclave. His guidance and expertise have been invaluable to all the participants. Prizes were distributed by our chief guest to all winners of different activities conducted in the conclave.

OUR SOURCES :

Daily Learning Session (DLS) :

• It is a session which happens on a daily basis where like minded aspirants come together and share their knowledge.


• Mentors are assigned to the respective subject(i.e. General Awareness, Current Affairs, Aptitude, Reasoning, English) by means of their interest to go deep in the topics and share valuable knowledge to the aspirants.

Code of Conduct (नीतिशास्त्र CoC) :

• It is a rulebook precisely a Constitution which will help CEC to run administration smoothly. The main intention of Niti Shastra is to distribute the powers between the members of CEC and also disintegration of power so that a single member will never have authority to take biased decisions rather, democracy will be followed while taking any decision.

• The benefit for students is that CEC will set a mark for discipline and this should be inherited by students is the only expectation.

Quiz :

• Social Media like Instagram and Telegram are used by the team to put Quizzes on the regular basis.The topics for the Quizzes are related to the various competitive exams like, Current Affairs, Geopolitical, History, Science, etc.

• Topics which are covered in the DLS of respective subject will get revised by the aspirants.

• Will generate habit of solving problems on a daily basis.

शास्त्रकोश (CEC Library) :

• ; CEC Library was formed on '10th Dec 2022' by the students for sharing their knowledge and resources they have with teach other.

• ; The purpose of forming the library was that aspirants preparing for different exams may get the valuable resources for their preparation and does not lack in their studies.CEC Library found to be beneficial for students in different competitive exams preparation.

Official Whatsapp Group :

• The next level of recruitment group of CEC to a group which provides platform for sharing knowledge related to all competitive exams.


• Activities includes Daily Newspaper, Vocabulary, Article of the day, Questions and solutions of DLS and sources of various subjects occurs in a group on a regular basis.

• Criteria to join this group is only consistency that one have to maintain during regular DLS and various events.

• This tests one's consistency and person approaching ability.It help aspirants to increase knowledge of different field i.e. technical, non-technical and social.
